A Burla demo · 2.76B rows in 15 seconds

Every NYC taxi trip ever released, classified by what happened to its neighborhood.

We processed 2,758,715,765 NYC taxi & for-hire trips across 168 months on Burla and sorted every one of the 264 TLC taxi zones into six categories — stable, emergent, cooling, warming, ghost, resurrected — purely by what its own trip-per-month time series looks like. No curated "interesting zones" list. Just the data.

Headline
The biggest emergent zone is Far Rockaway — 43.9 trips/month in 2011, 42,053 trips/month today. A 958× ride-volume explosion.
All twelve top-emergent zones are in the outer boroughs. Uber/Lyft literally built a second taxi system where yellow cabs had refused to go.

A few things we didn't expect

Ghost

Central Park, 40% of peak

From 219,763 trips/mo in May 2011 to 86,952/mo today. Classic Manhattan yellow-cab cannibalization by FHV — the neighborhood is still alive, the yellow-cab line isn't.

Emergent

Brownsville, 769× growth

156 trips/mo at birth → 120,262 recent. The outer boroughs aren't gentrifying; they're being served by a different taxi system for the first time.

Resurrected

LaGuardia, 82% back

From 609K trips/mo pre-pandemic → 10,717 in April 2020 → 502,846 today. Airports are back. Midtown (64–66% back) isn't. That's remote work, in one number.
